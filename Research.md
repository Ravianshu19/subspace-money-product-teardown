# Subspace.money Product Research

## Objective

The objective of this research was to evaluate Subspace.money from a Product Management perspective by analyzing the user experience, product positioning, trust mechanisms, monetization strategy, and overall user journey.

The analysis was conducted using real product exploration, screenshots, competitor benchmarking, and UX observation.

---

# Product Overview

Subspace.money is a consumer platform focused on:

* Subscription sharing
* Gift cards
* Credit card rewards
* Wallet services
* Group payments
* Rentals
* Community-based commerce

The platform targets price-sensitive Indian users looking to reduce spending through shared access, discounts, and financial optimization.

---

# Research Methodology

## 1. Product Exploration

The application was explored as a first-time user.

Screens analyzed:

* Home Page
* Explore Page
* Wallet
* Chat
* Account
* KYC Flow

## 2. Frameworks Used

### SWOT Analysis

Strengths:

* Strong savings-focused positioning
* Attractive dark-themed UI
* Multiple financial utility features
* Integrated wallet ecosystem

Weaknesses:

* Unclear primary value proposition
* Empty-state UX issues
* Limited onboarding guidance
* Trust communication gaps

Opportunities:

* Household subscription management
* Group buying ecosystem
* Social commerce
* Credit card optimization tools

Threats:

* OTT anti-sharing policies
* Fintech compliance regulations
* Competition from Paytm, CRED, Splitwise, and Fleek

# User Journey Analysis

## Stage 1: Discovery

Users land on the Home or Explore page.

Positive:

* Modern visual design
* Strong discount visibility

Issues:

* Multiple offerings compete for attention
* Users may not immediately understand the core value proposition

Observation:
The platform simultaneously promotes gift cards, credit card rewards, rentals, subscriptions, and payments.

Risk:
Users may struggle to understand what Subspace primarily solves.

---

## Stage 2: Exploration

Users browse categories and brands.

Positive:

* Large brand coverage
* Strong discount visibility

Issues:

* No personalized recommendations
* No trending products section
* No social proof indicators

Risk:
Choice overload may increase abandonment.

---

## Stage 3: Wallet Activation

Users are encouraged to add money.

Observed:

* Empty wallet state
* No transaction history
* No contextual education

Problem:
Users see a financial action before understanding the value.

Recommendation:
Introduce:

* Cashback examples
* Reward explanations
* First transaction bonus
* Interactive onboarding

---

## Stage 4: Trust Building

The Account page prominently displays KYC.

Observed:
Large KYC banner with minimal explanation.

Problem:
Users often perceive KYC as friction.

Recommendation:
Communicate benefits:

* Fraud protection
* Verified seller badge
* Faster withdrawals
* Higher transaction limits

---

## Stage 5: Community & Engagement

Chat functionality appears isolated.

Observed:
"No conversations found" empty state.

Problem:
Users do not understand:

* Why chat exists
* How to start using it
* What value it provides

Recommendation:
Add:

* Suggested groups
* Create group CTA
* Invite friends flow
* Recommended communities

# Key Product Insights

## Insight 1

Subspace's biggest strength is localized financial infrastructure.

The wallet, UPI integration, and savings ecosystem create strong market relevance for Indian consumers.

---

## Insight 2

Trust is the largest product moat.

Users must feel safe while sharing subscriptions, making payments, and joining groups.

Trust improvements can directly improve retention and conversion.

---

## Insight 3

The platform currently behaves like multiple products inside one application.

Users see:

* Wallet
* Payments
* Gift Cards
* Rentals
* Rewards
* Community Features

A stronger product hierarchy would improve clarity.

---

## Insight 4

Empty states represent major UX opportunities.

Wallet, Chat, and transaction screens currently provide little guidance for first-time users.

These screens can be converted into activation funnels.

Increase first transaction rate.

### Chat Onboarding

Goal:

### Product Positioning Clarity

Goal:
Improve user understanding of the platform.

---

## Medium Priority

### Personalized Discovery

Goal:
Reduce choice overload.

### KYC Trust Layer

Goal:
Increase trust and completion rates.

---

# Final Recommendation

Subspace.money has strong potential because it combines savings, payments, subscriptions, and rewards into a single ecosystem.

The next phase of growth should focus on:

1. Trust
2. Product clarity
3. User activation
4. Community engagement

By improving onboarding, reducing confusion, and turning empty states into educational experiences, Subspace can significantly improve retention and conversion while strengthening its position in India's growing subscription and savings economy.
